VAT in UAE
Businesses with taxable supplies or imports exceeding AED 375,000 per annum must mandatorily register for VAT. Voluntary registration is available if turnover exceeds AED 187,500. RR Associates will assess your threshold and guide you through the registration process.
Most businesses in the UAE file VAT returns quarterly. However, businesses with annual turnover exceeding AED 150 million are required to file monthly. The FTA assigns your tax period during registration. Late filing attracts administrative penalties.
The standard VAT rate in the UAE is 5%. Certain supplies such as healthcare, education, international transport, and residential properties may be zero-rated (0%). Some supplies are exempt, meaning no VAT is charged and input VAT cannot be recovered.
Missing the VAT filing deadline results in administrative penalties from the FTA, starting at AED 1,000 for the first offence and AED 2,000 for subsequent offences within 24 months. Additional penalties apply for late payment of VAT due. Our team ensures you never miss a deadline.
UAE Corporate Tax
The UAE Corporate Tax is levied at 0% on taxable income up to AED 375,000 and 9% on taxable income above AED 375,000. Multinational groups subject to the OECD Pillar Two rules may be subject to a minimum effective tax rate of 15% under the Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT).
Qualifying Free Zone Persons (QFZPs) may benefit from a 0% Corporate Tax rate on their qualifying income. However, they must satisfy strict substance requirements, maintain adequate presence in the free zone, and not earn income from non-qualifying activities. RR Associates can assess your QFZP eligibility.
UAE Corporate Tax applies to financial years beginning on or after 1 June 2023. The timing of your first tax period and filing deadline depends on your financial year-end. Most businesses with a calendar year-end had their first CT filing deadline in September 2025. Contact us to confirm your specific deadlines.
Accounting & Audit
Most free zone entities in the UAE are required to submit audited financial statements annually as a condition of their license renewal. Mainland LLCs are also required to maintain audited accounts under the UAE Commercial Companies Law. We can confirm the specific requirement for your entity type.
